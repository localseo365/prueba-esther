During the quarantine period on another day of boredom, I proposed a challenge to two close friends: A 24-hour gamejam! While my friends focused on coding, I led the creative direction, including game mechanics, art, level design, sound effects, and music composition. The game was inspired by a video of two esports players competing to type quickly on a website. The resulting horror-themed game incentivizes fast typing under pressure, with the player needing to save children to maintain health, because if you run out of them, the monster will take you and end the game.
The project was developed entirely with open-source programs, including two that were online webs!





The level design is very simple and is based on modular rooms, and a corridor in the center. Also a modular bridge to link extra rooms. If you want to advance to the next room you will have to solve the small puzzle to gain the code access and open the typewritten terminals. The writing action happens in the central room, while the puzzles and easter eggs are on the sides.
